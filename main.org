  <br/><br/>
 <pre>  ...There can be no doubt.
     Thanks to technology, we can be certain that the music of the past,
     like the music that is yet to come,
     will be music that has never been heard before...
   </pre>
   <center><h2><b><i><u>"Terretektorh 1965"</u></i></b></h2><a href="[[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=37ajOyhcl_c]]" target="_blank">

 </a></center>


  <br/>
<pre>  ...To make music means to express human intelligence by sonic means.
     This is intelligence in its broadest sense,
     which includes not only the peregrinations of pure logic
     but also the "logic" of emotions and intuition.
     My musical techniques, although often rigorous in their internal structure,
     leave many openings through which the most complex
     and mysterious factors of the intelligence may penetrate...
   </pre>
<center><h2><b><i><u>"Eonta 1964"</u></i></b></h2><a href="[[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IzUPAMY2A8k]]" target="_blank">
<img src="/eonta.jpg" alt="Eonta 1964" style="width:170px;height:202px;" /></a></center>



   <br/>
<pre>  ...Art, and, above all, music, has a fundamental function,
     which is to catalyze the sublimation that it can bring about through all means of expression.
     It must aim through fixations which are landmarks,
     to draw [one] towards a total exaltation in which the individual mingles,
     losing his consciousness in a truth immediate, rare, enormous, and perfect.
     If a work of art succeeds in this undertaking even for a single moment, it attains its goal...
   </pre>
   <center><h2><b><i><u>"PLeiadeS 1979"</u></i></b></h2><a href="[[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dqtFGaHcWRk]]" target="_blank">
<img src="/pleiades.jpg" alt="Pleiades 1979" style="width:170px;height:202px;" /></a></center>


   <br/>
 <pre>  ...The listener must be gripped and whether he likes it or not,
     drawn into the flight path of the sounds without special training being necessary.
     The sensual shock must be just as forceful as when one hears
     a clap of thunder or looks into a bottomless abyss...
  </pre>
 <center><h2><b><i><u>"Synaphai 1969"</u></i></b></h2><a href="[[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9pBMxp8EJFA]]" target="_blank">
<img src="/synaphai.jpg" alt="Synaphai 1969" style="width:170px;height:202px;" /></a></center>

   <br/>
<pre>  ...However if the mental constructs are in general expedient to accept
     the usefulness of geometry in plastic arts like architecture, painting etc,
     there is no choice remaining but only one stage to cover
     in order to accept that somebody can use non visual mathematics,
     even more abstract and or even machines in order to contribute to musical composition,
     which is even more abstract than plastic arts...

</pre>
